Some key points from our side:
1. We are currently testing clearer labels such as:
    ◦ “Parent App Activity” instead of “Inactive Parents”
    ◦ “Family App Activity” instead of “Fully Inactive Families”
Similarly, some legend labels are also being adjusted to better match the actual graph logic being shown.

2. One graph where I am still slightly unsure from a naming/usability perspective is:
“*Both Guardians on App*”
Technically the graph is correct, but the donut currently represents both:
    ◦ families where both guardians are on the app,
    ◦ and families where only one guardian is on the app.
So I wanted to check whether there may be a more business-friendly/end-user-friendly label for this graph as well.

3. Currently the dashboard displays 8 graphs together in a single grid view. From a usability perspective, the screen can feel slightly dense for first-time users.
For example:
    ◦ family-related graphs currently appear across different areas of the dashboard,
    ◦ “Families”, “Families (Incoming)”, and the family activity graph are conceptually related but visually separated.
Potentially grouping related graphs closer together, or introducing slightly more sectional separation/another row, may improve readability and scanning.

4. During implementation we also clarified and aligned the backend logic for the new activity graphs:
*"Parent App Activity"* = active vs inactive app-user parents within the last 30 days.
*"Family App Activity"* = if at least one guardian in a family is recently active, the family is treated as *active/partially active*; *only families with no active guardians* are treated as *fully inactive*.

We went with this approach because it felt more meaningful from both engagement and end-user interpretation perspectives.

5. Looking slightly ahead as well:
currently inactive users open a dedicated *list view,* but if more activity-related drilldowns are added in future, it may eventually make sense to think about a more centralized engagement dashboard or a table that shows  parents, family  or children, otherwise filtering approach in dashboard instead of multiple separate screens/views.

Just to clarify as well :slightly_smiling_face:-  I am definitely not trying to suggest large redesign/rework here. These were mainly observations that came up during testing and discussion from an end-user perspective, and I felt it would be better to raise them early before the dashboard structure becomes more fixed later.

Please let us know your thoughts on:
• graph naming/labeling,
• grouping/layout,
• and overall usability from the end-user perspective.
